# VAT Rules (TVA — Taxe sur la Valeur Ajoutée)

## Rates — France mainland (Métropole)

| Rate | Name                         | Applies to                                                        |
|------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 20%  | Normal                       | Most goods and services                                           |
| 10%  | Intermediate (intermédiaire) | Restaurants, passenger transport, renovation works, accommodation |
| 5.5% | Reduced (réduit)             | Food products, books, energy, cultural events, adapted housing    |
| 2.1% | Super-reduced (particulier)  | Regulated press, reimbursed medications                           |

## Rates — Corsica (Corse)

| Rate | Applies to                                                     |
|------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| 20%  | Standard rate (same as mainland)                               |
| 13%  | Petroleum products                                             |
| 10%  | Certain renovation works, on-premise consumption               |
| 2.1% | Water, non-alcoholic beverages, public transport               |
| 0.9% | Live show admissions, live animal sales to non-taxable persons |

## Rates — Overseas Departments (DOM)

### Guadeloupe, Martinique, La Réunion
| Rate  | Applies to                                  |
|-------|---------------------------------------------|
| 8.5%  | Normal rate                                 |
| 2.1%  | Reduced rate (food, press, essential goods) |
| 1.75% | Certain specific products                   |
| 1.05% | Some essential items                        |

### Guyane, Mayotte
- **No VAT** — fully exempt territories

## Franchise en base (VAT exemption for small businesses)

### Thresholds (2026)
| Activity              | Standard threshold | Increased threshold |
|-----------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Services              | 37,500 EUR         | 41,250 EUR          |
| Goods (achat/revente) | 85,000 EUR         | 93,500 EUR          |

### Rules
- Below standard threshold: exempt, no VAT charged
- Between standard and increased: exempt until end of current year
- Above increased threshold: VAT applies **immediately** from the
  transaction that crosses the threshold
- **Mandatory mention on invoices**: "TVA non applicable — article 293 B du CGI"
- Cannot deduct VAT on purchases while exempt

## Reverse charge (Autoliquidation)

### When it applies
- Intra-EU B2B transactions (goods and services)
- Certain domestic subcontracting in construction
- Imports

### Rules
- Seller does NOT charge VAT
- Buyer accounts for VAT in their own declaration (self-assessment)
- **Mandatory mention**: "Autoliquidation — article 283-2 du CGI"
- Both parties must have valid intra-EU VAT numbers

## VAT on down payments

Since **1 January 2023**:
- VAT is due upon receipt of any down payment
- Applies to both goods deliveries AND service provisions
- The down payment invoice must show the VAT amount
- Previously, services could defer VAT to completion — no longer

## Invoicing between VAT regimes

| Seller            | Buyer           | VAT on invoice                    |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------------------------|
| Standard regime   | Standard regime | Yes, seller's rate                |
| Franchise en base | Any             | No VAT, mention art. 293 B        |
| EU seller (B2B)   | French buyer    | No VAT, reverse charge            |
| French seller     | EU buyer (B2B)  | No VAT, reverse charge            |
| French seller     | Non-EU buyer    | No VAT, export exemption art. 262 |
