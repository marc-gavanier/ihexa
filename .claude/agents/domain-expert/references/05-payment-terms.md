# Payment Terms and Late Penalties (Délais de paiement et pénalités)

## Legal basis

- Code de commerce: Articles L441-10 to L441-16
- Sanctions: up to 75,000 EUR (individual) / 2,000,000 EUR (company)

## Payment deadlines

### Default rule
- **30 days** from goods receipt or service completion

### Maximum allowed
- **60 days** from invoice date, OR
- **45 days end-of-month** (45 jours fin de mois) from invoice date
- Parties can agree on either formula, but cannot exceed these limits

### Exceptions
- Seasonal industries may have specific agreements
- Transport sector: 30 days maximum
- Fresh/perishable food: 30 days (some products: 20 days)

### What counts as the start date
- For goods: date of receipt
- For services: date of completion
- For invoices: date of issue (when using the 60-day rule)

## Late payment penalties (Pénalités de retard)

### Rate
- **Minimum**: 3 times the legal interest rate (taux d'intérêt légal)
  - 2026 value: approximately **7.86%** per year
- **Recommended by law**: ECB refinancing rate + 10 points
  - Usually around **14-15%** per year
- The rate chosen must appear on invoices and in CGV

### Application
- Apply **automatically** from the day after the due date
- **No formal notice required** (no mise en demeure needed)
- Calculated per day of delay
- Formula: `(amount due × penalty rate × days late) / 365`

### Example
Invoice of 10,000 EUR, 30 days late, at 12.15% rate:
`10,000 × 0.1215 × 30 / 365 = 99.86 EUR`

## Fixed recovery fee (Indemnité forfaitaire de recouvrement)

- **Amount**: 40 EUR per unpaid invoice
- Applied **once** per late invoice (not per day)
- Due automatically, same as penalties — no formal notice required
- If actual recovery costs exceed 40 EUR, the creditor can claim
  the real amount (with justification)
- Must be mentioned on every invoice

## Dunning process (Procédure de recouvrement)

### Phase 1: Friendly reminder (Relance amiable)
- Not legally required but standard practice
- Phone call and/or written reminder
- No specific form required
- Recommended: keep written trace

### Phase 2: Formal notice (Mise en demeure)
- **Must** be sent by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt
  (lettre recommandée avec accusé de réception) OR by bailiff (huissier)
- Constitutes proof of notification
- Triggers legal deadline for debtor to respond
- Marks the start of potential interest claims in court

### Phase 3: Judicial proceedings
- **Injonction de payer** (payment order):
  - Simplified court procedure
  - Creditor files a request with supporting documents
  - Judge issues an order without hearing the debtor
  - Debtor can oppose within 1 month
- **Référé** (summary proceedings): faster, for undisputed debts
- **Procédure au fond** (full trial): for contested debts

### Prescription (statute of limitations)
- **B2B debts**: 5 years
- **B2C debts**: 2 years
